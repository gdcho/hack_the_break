About

The Glance Imposter Phenomenon Scale (CIPS) was created by Pauline Rose Clance with the intention of aiding individuals in identifying whether they exhibit characteristics of the Imposter Phenomenon (IP) and, if so, to what degree they are experiencing it.

Clance recommends taking the IP assessment solely for personal purposes and adhering to the provided guidelines. It should be noted that the results of this test are not an official diagnosis. However, in the event of any concerns regarding the outcome of the test, it is suggested to explore the IP literature available on this platform, consider obtaining Clance's IP book for further information, or seek counseling from a local mental health provider.

Note. From The Impostor Phenomenon: When Success Makes You Feel Like A Fake (pp. 20-22), by P.R. Clance, 1985, Toronto: Bantam Books. Copyright 1985 by Pauline Rose Clance, Ph.D., ABPP. Reprinted by permission. Do not reproduce without permission from Pauline Rose Clance.
